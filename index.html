Module One

Historical Foundation

1783

John Michell's Dark Stars

The Reverend John Michell became the first to propose that gravity could trap light. Using Newton's corpuscular theory—treating light as particles with mass—he calculated the conditions for an object whose escape velocity exceeds light speed.

Escape Velocity
vesc = √(2GM/r)
If vesc ≥ c, light cannot escape

"All light emitted from such a body would be made to return towards it, by its own proper gravity."

— John Michell, 1784
1915

Einstein's General Relativity

Einstein revolutionized physics by describing gravity not as a force, but as the curvature of spacetime itself. Mass and energy tell space how to curve; curved space tells matter how to move.

Field Equations
Gμν + Λgμν = (8πG/c⁴)Tμν
Geometry = Energy Content
Key Insight

"Space-time tells matter how to move; matter tells space-time how to curve."

— John Wheeler
1916

Schwarzschild's Solution

From the trenches of WWI, Karl Schwarzschild derived the first exact solution to Einstein's equations. He found the spacetime geometry around a spherical mass—revealing the event horizon.

Schwarzschild Radius
Rs = 2GM/c²
The point of no return

"As you see, the war treated me kindly enough... to allow me to get away from it all and take this walk in the land of your ideas."

— Schwarzschild to Einstein, 1915

The Singularity

A point (Schwarzschild) or ring (Kerr) where curvature becomes infinite. This isn't a physical object—it signals where General Relativity breaks down.

The Event Horizon

The defining boundary at r = Rs. Acts as a one-way membrane: matter and information flow in, nothing flows out.

The Accretion Disk

Superheated matter spiraling inward at relativistic speeds. Friction heats gas to millions of degrees, emitting X-rays visible across the cosmos.

Fundamental Theorem

The No-Hair Theorem

Black holes are remarkably simple objects. Despite the complexity of what falls in, a black hole is completely described by just three numbers. All other information is "lost".

The "missing link". Elusive objects potentially formed from stellar mergers in clusters.

Module Five

Thermodynamics

Hawking Radiation

Stephen Hawking's 1974 breakthrough: black holes aren't perfectly black. Quantum fluctuations near the horizon create particle pairs, causing the black hole to slowly evaporate.

TH = ℏc³/(8πGMkB)

Entropy

Jacob Bekenstein proposed that black hole entropy is proportional to horizon area—not volume. This suggests information is stored on the 2D surface.

S = kBc³A/(4Gℏ)

The Information Paradox

If black holes evaporate, what happens to the information of the matter that fell in? Quantum mechanics says information cannot be destroyed. General Relativity suggests it is. This is the biggest open problem in modern physics.

Firewall

A wall of high energy particles at the horizon burns anything that tries to enter.

Wormholes
